# AA Question Bank Gap Summary

The audit found that the original headline total of 1,328 stored questions represented only 166 genuinely distinct mathematical task templates: two core blueprints per syllabus point were repeatedly wrapped as exam, MCQ and cloze forms. This was the principal quality and coverage weakness.

Other baseline gaps were:

- no mixed-topic questions;
- no rendered question diagrams in the final loaded banks;
- no difficulty-4 classification;
- 720 failures across 1,900 randomized generator samples, caused by obsolete syllabus mappings and repeated distractors;
- missing requested metadata for question families, secondary topics, styles and validation status;
- no interface filters for mixed-topic status, representation or the highest difficulty;
- inadequate diversity within most individual syllabus points despite high stored counts.

The first strategic expansion batch addresses high-value natural combinations across functions, calculus, trigonometry, finance, probability, statistics, vectors, complex numbers, geometry, numerical methods and series. It also establishes reusable metadata, rendering and validation infrastructure for continued expansion.

The bank is not yet at the stated 500-distinct-template objective. The after-audit counts 179 distinct templates, leaving 321 genuinely new families to create. The highest-priority remaining areas are listed programmatically in `aa_coverage_comparison.md`; they are concentrated in AHL complex-number basics, polynomial and rational functions, odd/even and modulus functions, reciprocal/further trigonometric graphs, vector lines and planes, continuous distributions, first principles, implicit differentiation and advanced integration.
